Wearable 2030

Wearables can help physical advancement for athletes, and Parkinson's disease patients, etc. More Apple Watch apps that support 3D motions will come in coming years, and we can get the pattern data of all kinds of movements. But the battery is one of the significant issues in the wearables.

At Northeastern. a few electrical engineering and mechanical engineering kids were writing about the maintenance-free power sources, Thermoelectric. This electronic generator allows power generation from the heat. The cool idea is to generate power from the heat on humans. Your wrist is warmer when you wear a watch, right? But material scientists are still trying to improve the efficiency of Thermoelectric electrical generators. Another issue with Thermoelectric is their rigidity and bulkiness, which prevents them from being practical. Mechatronics is an exciting field for Thermoelectric and wearables. If anyone has good online learning material, lmk. Mechatronics is like an interaction of computer science, electrical engineering, and mechanical engineering. MATRIX Industries is one of the smartwatch examples which doesn't need a charger. There are more ways of saving battery and charge. One of which is battery swapping. We used to hold more sentimental value to electrical products. But we don't do as much anymore. As our software doesn't stores data locally as much, the concept of swapping can be a method for smartphones and wearables. There are more than 300 EV companies in China, one of the leaders, NIO, Aulton & Geely uses the technique of battery swapping. As of today, NIO operates more than 500 swapping stations. Our wearables's battery or the device itself could also be swaped in coming years or be rented out for a temporary use.

Near Field Communications(NFC) are what you find inside Apple Pay and metropolitan metro cards. 20 cents per tag. A tag can hold mini information and transmit it to the receiver. If you want, you don't need an Apple watch to use your card, and you can stick a NFC tag in the back of Rolex Deepsea and use it as a metro card. Shapes and concepts of wearable are changing every day. I want to intergrade NFC into my app to make a business card app or app alternative to USB. But it is so complex, and please contact me if you know how-to or know someone who knows it.

Another change is the material of wearables. Bluetooth Low Energy allowed the mass production of inexpensive wearables. But there are so many plastics. Some companies are shifting to Biodegradable wearables.
Lastly, werables will be not so werable as we start to insert devices inside our body. we advanced to insert needles and plates to fix our bones and bodies. Pace maker was developed in 50s. I see it as an exciting tool to gain superpower.
